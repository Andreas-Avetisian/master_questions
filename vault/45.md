# Provide and explain the eight strategies proposed by ENISA for implementing Privacy by Design in practice.

ENISA translates Privacy by Design into eight practical strategies. The core idea is that privacy should be built into the system architecture and development process from the start, not added afterwards.

## The 8 Strategies

- **MINIMISE:** collect and process only the data that is actually necessary. This follows the GDPR principle of data minimization.
- **HIDE:** keep personal data and the links between personal data hidden as much as possible.
- **SEPARATE:** process personal data separately or in a distributed way where possible, so that everything is not concentrated in one place.
- **AGGREGATE:** use the highest possible level of aggregation that still allows the intended processing.
- **INFORM:** make sure data subjects are informed when their data is processed and understand what is happening.
- **CONTROL:** give data subjects control over their data, for example through consent, correction, or deletion rights.
- **ENFORCE:** ensure that data-protection rules are actually implemented and enforced inside the system and organisation.
- **DEMONSTRATE:** make compliance verifiable through documentation, TOMs, and similar evidence.

## Why They Matter

These strategies turn the abstract idea of Privacy by Design into concrete design guidance. In short, they tell developers to collect less, reveal less, connect less, empower users more, and make compliance provable.
