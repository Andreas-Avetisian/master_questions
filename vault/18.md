# What is Overfitting? What can you do against it?

## What Overfitting is

A model is **overfitting** when it learns the *training data too well*, including its noise and idiosyncrasies, and therefore fails to generalise to new data.

Signal: **training performance is good, validation / test performance is markedly worse**. In bias / variance terms, overfitting = **high variance, low bias**. Its opposite is underfitting (model too simple, high bias, low variance).

Typical causes:

- Model too complex (too many layers, too many parameters) for the amount of data.
- Too few training samples.
- Training for too many epochs.
- Insufficient regularisation.
- Data leakage, or a training set that does not represent reality.

## What to do against it

From the AI course's "if you are overfitting you can" list and the regularisation overview:

**1. Regularise the model.** Add a penalty to the loss function so the weights cannot grow arbitrarily.

- **L1 (Lasso)**: penalty proportional to |weights|, pushes weights to zero, effectively feature selection.
- **L2 (Ridge)**: penalty proportional to weights², keeps weights small, most common default.
- **Elastic Net** (L1 + L2): combination of both.

**2. Early stopping.** Monitor validation loss after each epoch and stop as soon as it starts to rise again, before the model memorises the training set.

**3. Dropout.** During training, randomly zero out a fraction (typically 0.2 – 0.5) of neurons per batch. This forces the network to learn redundant, resilient representations and prevents neuron co-adaptation.

**4. Batch normalisation.** Normalise activations layer by layer; it speeds training *and* reduces overfitting by acting as a mild regulariser.

**5. More / better data.**

- Collect more samples (no regularisation substitutes for real data).
- **Data augmentation** (rotations, crops, noise for images; synonym / back-translation for text) to artificially enlarge the training set.
- **Normalisation** so the model does not fit to scale artefacts.

**6. Reduce model capacity.** Fewer layers / fewer neurons, simpler architecture, or train shorter.

**7. Cross-validation.** Use k-fold CV to detect overfitting reliably instead of relying on a single train / test split.

**8. Ensembling.** Bagging, random forests or averaging several models reduces variance.

## Short summary

Overfitting = the model memorises the training data, so training accuracy is high but validation / test accuracy drops. It is the high-variance side of the bias / variance tradeoff. Counter it with **regularisation (L1, L2, Elastic Net), early stopping, dropout, batch normalisation**, more / augmented data, a simpler model and cross-validation. If training and validation curves diverge, you are overfitting; if both are poor, you are underfitting.
