# You are supervising a survey paper in Cyber Security. Explain how correct citation practice supports not only academic honesty but also argumentation quality, traceability, and reader verification. Illustrate your answer with direct quotation, paraphrase, and figure/data citation cases.

Correct citation practice in a cyber-security survey supports four things at once:

- **Academic honesty:** you acknowledge others' ideas, wording, figures, and data instead of presenting them as your own.
- **Argumentation quality:** citations show which source supports which claim and how your argument is built.
- **Traceability:** readers can track down the original source if they want more detail.
- **Reader verification:** complete references let readers re-find the source and check whether you cited it fairly.

The PP bibliography slide adds that a reference entry should contain enough metadata to stay findable later, for example author, title, year, publication type, and DOI / ISBN / stable URL.

**Three citation cases:**

- **Direct quotation:** use quotation marks, keep the wording exact, and give the source plus page number. Example: quoting the exact wording of an RFC, CVE description, or attack model. Direct quotes should be rare.
- **Paraphrase:** restate the source in your own words, but still cite it. Rewording without citation is plagiarism, and wording that stays too close to the original is also problematic. This is the normal form in a survey because it enables comparison and synthesis.
- **Figures and data:** every non-original figure, table, diagram, or dataset needs a source line such as `Source: ...` or `Adapted from ...`. For changing datasets such as CVE / NVD snapshots, cite the version or date. If there is too much raw data, keep only relevant excerpts in the main text and refer to the appendix.

As a supervisor, I would check that every non-trivial claim is cited, direct quotes are page-numbered, paraphrases are genuine, figures and tables have source lines, and the bibliography is complete. If that is done well, the paper is not just honest, but also argued, traceable, and verifiable.
