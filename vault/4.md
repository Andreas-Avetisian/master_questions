# When do we need to create samples in market research? Please explain the difference between a full census and a partial survey and give an example for each of the two.

## When do we need a sample?

Before surveying anything, ask three questions:

1. Do I even need a sample?
2. If yes, how can it represent my basic population (representativity)?
3. What is actually achievable?

A sample is needed whenever surveying the entire basic population is impossible, too expensive, or too slow. If the population is small, accessible, and affordable to survey completely, a sample is not needed.

> **Basic population:** the entirety of a group of market participants that can be considered as the object of investigation.

## Full census vs. partial survey

- **Full census:** all members of a real, statistical mass are included in the study.
- **Partial survey:** only a certain number of units from the population are selected; the selected units form the **sample**.

## Examples

- **Full census — Example 1:** evaluating how satisfied the first-semester students of this master are with the "Methods of Research and Innovation" course. The population is small (a single cohort) and reachable, so everyone can be surveyed directly.
- **Partial survey — Example 2:** evaluating how the population of Lower Austria thinks about AI safety. The population is too large to survey completely, so a representative sample is drawn instead.
