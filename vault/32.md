# Define scientific research and discuss the core criteria that an activity must fulfill in order to be classified as scientific research.

## Definition of scientific research

The lecture gives two complementary definitions.

**General working definition**: research and science are *"a systematic (planned) endeavour, that searches, builds and organizes (new) knowledge."*

**Frascati Manual 2015 (OECD)**: *"Research and experimental development (R&D) comprise creative and systematic work undertaken in order to increase the stock of knowledge, including knowledge of humankind, culture and society, and to devise new applications of available knowledge."*

**EDPS (European Data Protection Supervisor)** adds the methodological angle: *"scientific research applies the 'scientific method' of observing phenomena, formulating, and testing a hypothesis for those phenomena, and concluding as to the validity of the hypothesis. The conduct of research must allow testing of hypotheses, with both the conclusion and the reasoning transparent and open to criticism. Openness and transparency help distinguish between science and pseudo-science."*

Taken together, scientific research is a *planned, creative, transparent, hypothesis-driven production of new knowledge*, carried out in a way that others can scrutinise and reproduce.

## The five core criteria (Frascati Manual 2015, p. 46 ff)

An activity qualifies as R&D, and thus as scientific research, only if it satisfies **all five** of the following criteria:

1. **Novel**: the activity must aim at *new* findings. Routine work or mere application of existing knowledge is not research.
2. **Creative**: it must rest on original, non-obvious concepts and hypotheses. Purely mechanical or repetitive work does not qualify.
3. **Uncertain**: the outcome, the required resources or the time needed must be uncertain at the outset. If the result is predictable in advance, the activity is not research.
4. **Systematic**: the work must be planned and documented, with a clear research question, method and record of the process and results.
5. **Transferable and/or reproducible**: the findings must be shareable, so that other researchers can use, verify or reproduce them. This is what links the activity to the wider body of scientific knowledge.

If any one of these criteria is missing, the activity falls outside the scope of scientific research (it may still be useful work, but it is not R&D in the OECD sense).

## Types this definition covers

The same Frascati Manual distinguishes three kinds of R&D activity that all satisfy the five criteria: **basic research** (new knowledge of underlying phenomena without a specific application), **applied research** (new knowledge directed at a practical aim), and **experimental development** (systematic use of existing knowledge to produce or improve products and processes).

## Short summary

Scientific research is a systematic, creative and hypothesis-driven endeavour that produces new knowledge and is transparent enough to be tested and reproduced by others. For an activity to count as scientific research in the OECD/Frascati sense it must be **novel, creative, uncertain, systematic, and transferable/reproducible**, all five at the same time. The EDPS complements this by requiring the scientific method, and openness to criticism, as the line between science and pseudo-science. Research itself comes in three flavours: basic, applied, and experimental development.
