# Describe the main steps of a usability test. Why is it important to test your solution with users?

A usability test is usually a 1:1 test with real users to see whether they can complete relevant tasks with the product.

## Main steps of a usability test

1. Define the research goals and usability questions.
   Clarify what you want to learn, for example whether users can find information, navigate intuitively, complete the workflow, and how satisfied they feel.
2. Prepare the test.
   Create realistic scenarios and tasks, prepare questions, schedule the sessions, run a pilot test, and check the test environment and equipment.
3. Recruit representative test persons.
   Select users from the target group so the results are meaningful.
4. Brief the participant.
   Welcome them, explain the procedure, collect consent/background information, and introduce methods such as thinking aloud.
5. Run the tasks and observe.
   Give tasks step by step, watch how the participant works, record time, problems, comments, and other critical data, and avoid helping too much.
6. Collect post-test feedback.
   Use a post-test questionnaire and debriefing to capture perceptions, confusion, likes, dislikes, and ideas.
7. Analyse the results and derive improvements.
   Compare the findings with the usability goals, especially effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction, identify why problems occurred, and use this to improve the design.

## Why it is important to test with users

- It shows whether people can actually use the solution, not just whether the team thinks it should work.
- It uncovers usability problems, wrong assumptions, and points where users get confused.
- It reveals why usability goals are not achieved and what should be changed.
- It helps avoid building the wrong thing and supports evidence-based iteration.

In short: testing with users validates the solution in real use and provides concrete input for improving it.
