# Name and describe the typical contents of a technical specification.

Typical contents of a **technical specification** are:

- **Introduction / objective:** states what is to be achieved.
- **People involved / roles:** identifies who is responsible and who is involved.
- **Initial situation:** describes the current status, existing systems, and known problems.
- **Detailed requirements:** includes both **functional requirements** (what the system should do) and **non-functional requirements** (for example performance or security).
- **System architecture / technical framework conditions:** explains the technical environment and structure.
- **Data flows and processes:** describes workflows, data structures, and diagrams.
- **Test concept / acceptance criteria:** explains how correctness is checked and when the result is accepted.
- **Project planning (optional):** may include milestones, responsibilities, and time frame.
- **Glossary and appendices:** define terms and include references or additional documents.
