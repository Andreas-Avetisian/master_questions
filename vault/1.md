# Name the three research types and describe them. What is the difference between those three? Provide at least three examples for typical rough classifications between research fields. Explain theoretical and empirical work.

## Three research types

- **Basic (fundamental) research:** expands the knowledge base and theories of a discipline, tests and refines them, and does **not** primarily pursue a concrete practical goal.
- **Applied research:** expands and uses research results in order to solve a **specific practical problem**.
- **Experimental development:** systematically applies existing knowledge to create **new or substantially improved** materials, devices, products, processes, or systems.

## Difference Between The Three

The main difference is the **goal** of the work:

- **Basic research** aims at new understanding.
- **Applied research** aims at solving a practical problem.
- **Experimental development** aims at building or improving a concrete solution.

Short progression:

`basic research -> applied research -> experimental development`

In practice, the boundaries are often **fluid** and depend on the research field and object.

## Rough Classifications Of Research Fields

- **Natural sciences:** research on animate and inanimate nature.
  Example: physics, chemistry, biology.
- **Humanities:** research on cultural and intellectual/spiritual subjects.
  Example: history, philosophy, linguistics.
- **Social sciences:** research on human social coexistence.
  Example: sociology, political science, economics.
- **Engineering sciences:** research on the development and construction of material products.
  Example: mechanical engineering, civil engineering, electrical engineering.
- **Structural / formal sciences:** research on immaterial systems and products created by humans.
  Example: mathematics, logic, theoretical computer science.

Important: these are only **rough** classifications. Some cases are debatable, especially fields like **mathematics** and **computer science**.

## Theoretical vs. Empirical Work

- **Theoretical work:** answers a scientific question mainly by working with relevant literature, concepts, models, and theories.
  Typical tasks: developing models, comparing theories, validating concepts theoretically.
- **Empirical work:** answers a scientific question through **theory-based collection, analysis, and interpretation of data**.
  Typical forms: case studies, surveys, observations, hypothesis testing.

## Core Distinction To Remember

- **Basic / applied / experimental development** classify research by its **purpose**.
- **Theoretical / empirical** classify research by **how knowledge is generated**.

So:

- **Theoretical** = knowledge mainly from conceptual/model-based work.
- **Empirical** = knowledge mainly from observation, measurement, or collected data.
