# Name and explain 4 different types and venues for non-academic publishing.

Non-academic publishing means communicating results **outside peer-reviewed academic papers**, usually for **practitioners, customers, industry partners, or the general public**.

Typical examples are:

- **Industry white papers:** structured documents published by a company or organisation, usually on a **company website, download portal, or as customer material**. They explain a technology, method, or solution in a practical and often persuasive way.
- **Trade magazines / professional journals:** articles published in **industry magazines or association journals**. They target practitioners and focus on trends, case studies, best practices, and practical relevance rather than academic novelty.
- **Blog posts / company tech blogs:** shorter online texts published on **company blogs, engineering blogs, or developer portals**. They are useful for quickly sharing experiences, implementation insights, lessons learned, and opinions with a broad audience.
- **Conference talks at industry events:** presentations given at **industry conferences, trade fairs, vendor events, or meetups**. They are a venue for sharing applied results directly with professionals, customers, and partners.

Other possible non-academic formats mentioned in this context are **newspaper articles, podcasts, patents, and standards**.
