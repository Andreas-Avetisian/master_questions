# Describe the common structure/chapters/sections of a thesis/paper! Describe briefly the expected content of each section/chapter!

There is no single universal structure for every thesis or paper, but the cited sources describe a common overall pattern. Papers usually compress it more strongly, while theses often expand it into full chapters.

## Common Structure

- **Abstract:** a short summary of the whole work, including topic, problem, approach, and main result.
- **Introduction:** motivates the topic, explains the problem, defines the scope, and often states the contribution and structure of the document.
- **Background / Preliminaries / Theoretical Background:** gives the concepts and prior knowledge needed to understand the work.
- **Related Work / State of the Art / Literature Review:** shows what is already known, how existing approaches differ, and where the gap is.
- **Methodology / Approach:** explains how the problem is tackled and why this method was chosen.
- **Implementation / Prototype / Experimental Setup:** describes the concrete realization or technical setup when applicable.
- **Results:** presents the findings, measurements, artefacts, or outputs of the work.
- **Evaluation / Discussion:** interprets the results, checks whether the goals were reached, and discusses strengths, weaknesses, and limitations.
- **Conclusion / Future Work:** summarizes the contribution and points to sensible next steps.
- **References / Bibliography:** lists the cited sources in the required citation style.
- **Appendix / Acknowledgements:** optional supporting material such as extra tables, questionnaires, code details, or thanks.

## Exam Formula

The usual logic is: first explain the problem and prior work, then present the method and results, then discuss what they mean, and finally close with the conclusion and references.
