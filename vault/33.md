# Critically examine the relationship between law and ethics. In your answer, clearly distinguish between the two concepts and explain their points of overlap and divergence.

Law and ethics are related, but they are not identical. Both are **normative disciplines** because they guide human behaviour and deal with what ought to be done. However, law is a specifically legal order created and enforced by the state, whereas ethics evaluates whether conduct is morally right or wrong.

**Distinction**
- **Law** consists of positive norms enacted by authorised state bodies in a formal procedure.
- It is backed by coercion: the state can attach sanctions such as fines or imprisonment.
- Following Max Weber, law is distinguished by a `Rechtsstab`, that is, an institutional apparatus that enforces compliance through coercion.
- **Ethics** is grounded in conscience, reason, and moral reflection rather than state enactment.
- Following Kant, ethics concerns a person's **inner attitude** and inner duty, while law mainly requires the **external conformity** of behaviour with legal rules.
- Ethical, religious, or social norms may also be sanctioned, but usually only informally through blame, contempt, or social pressure.

**Overlap**
- Law and ethics overlap because legal systems often embody moral ideas.
- The slides explicitly link law to values such as **justice, freedom, equality, and human dignity**.
- Ethics therefore influences the content and interpretation of law, especially in constitutional and human-rights contexts.

**Divergence**
- What is lawful is not always ethical, and what is ethical is not always lawful.
- The clearest example in the slides is **apartheid**: it was legally permitted, but morally wrong.
- This shows that formal legal validity does not automatically guarantee moral legitimacy.
- Radbruch's formula sharpens this point: law that is intolerably unjust and denies the basic equality of persons may count as **legal injustice** and lose its claim to obedience.

**Critical Assessment**
- The relationship is best understood as one of **partial overlap**, not identity.
- A separation between law and ethics is important because law needs legal certainty, formal validity, and enforceability.
- But a complete separation is dangerous, because it can make even deeply unjust rules appear acceptable merely because they were validly enacted.
- Ethics therefore serves as a critical standard for judging law, while law gives some ethical principles institutional form and enforceable effect.

**Exam Formula**
Law and ethics are both normative disciplines, but law is state-made and coercively enforced, while ethics is grounded in conscience and moral reasoning; they overlap in values such as justice and human dignity, yet diverge because legality and morality do not always coincide.
