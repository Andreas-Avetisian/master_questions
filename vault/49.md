# What is Data Cleansing and why is it used? Discuss the impact of different cleansing strategies. Discuss resilient Data Cleansing.

**Data cleansing** means making data usable according to the requirements of later processing. It is used to deal with data that is undefined, unspecified, uninterpretable, or unexpected, and generally to improve data quality or satisfy organisational and regulatory requirements.

## Why It Is Used

- remove or repair problematic records
- improve data quality before analysis or AI use
- make data fit the intended processing task
- support related steps such as enrichment, combination of streams, anonymization, or pseudonymization

## Impact Of Different Cleansing Strategies

The impact can be large. The slides describe experiments in data anonymization where different implementations produced different record counts on the same base data, with differences of up to **10%**. The reason was data cleansing.

This shows that cleansing is not neutral:

- dropping records changes the population being analysed
- changing values can change later statistics or classifications
- different cleansing rules can lead to different results
- impact analysis is especially difficult for NNs and DNNs

## Resilient Data Cleansing

The lecture argues that cleansing itself must be robust and traceable. **Resilient data cleansing** therefore includes:

- critical evaluation of the chosen cleansing strategy
- monitoring which records were dropped or changed
- **re-doability** of the cleansing step
- **un-doability** of cleansing decisions
- impact analysis for cleansing actions
- quality measures for judging the effect of cleansing

The proposed direction is strong logging and traceability, for example audit-and-control style logging, so that it is always clear which data was processed, when, by which step, and with which effect.

## Exam Formula

Data cleansing is necessary because raw data is often not directly usable, but cleansing can strongly influence later results. Therefore, resilient data cleansing requires transparent strategies, logging, reversibility, and impact analysis.
