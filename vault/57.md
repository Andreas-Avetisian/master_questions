# Trusted Computing: Give the definition of a "trusted system"! What does it say about a system's security? How can trust in something be assessed? Compare to common social interactions.

Trust in computing is first of all an **expectation of behaviour**.

## Definition

A **trusted system** is a system that **behaves in the expected manner for a particular purpose**.

## What This Says About Security

This does **not** automatically mean that the system is secure.

- trust is about expected behaviour
- security is about protection against threats and misuse
- a system can be trusted to behave as expected and still be insecure
- the slides also stress that trusted behaviour is not automatically morally good behaviour

## How Trust Can Be Assessed

According to the slides, trust can be assessed:

- when something can be **unambiguously identified**
- when it **operates unhindered**
- through **first-hand experience** of consistent good behaviour
- through **references** from already trusted parties
- ultimately through trust in the people or organisations behind it

## Comparison To Social Interactions

This is similar to normal social trust:

- we trust a person when we know who they are
- we trust them more after repeated reliable behaviour
- we may also trust them because someone already trusted recommends them

The same pattern appears in computing: identification, repeated reliable behaviour, and trusted references all help create trust.

## Exam Formula

A trusted system is one that behaves as expected for a given purpose. That says something about predictability, but not automatically about security. Trust is assessed through identification, experience, and trusted references, just as in ordinary human relationships.
