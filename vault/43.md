# AI-Act: Provide a short overview on its purpose. Describe the risk-based approach and provide 6 requirements for providers and 3 for users of high-risk AI systems.

The EU AI Act creates harmonised rules for the development, placing on the market, and use of AI in the EU. Its main aims are to ensure that AI systems on the EU market are safe and lawful, create legal certainty for innovation and investment, improve governance and enforcement, and support a single market for lawful, safe, and trustworthy AI.

## Risk-Based Approach

The AI Act follows a risk-based model, so the stricter the possible impact on safety and fundamental rights, the stricter the obligations.

- **Unacceptable risk:** prohibited entirely. The slides give examples such as social scoring by public authorities, manipulation or exploitation of vulnerable groups, and real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces for law enforcement.
- **High risk:** subject to the strongest requirements. This includes AI that is part of certain regulated products and AI systems listed in Annex III, such as automated hiring or biometric identification systems.
- **Limited risk:** mainly subject to transparency duties, for example chatbots, deep fakes, and emotion-recognition systems.
- **Minimal risk:** generally unregulated, although voluntary codes of conduct are encouraged.

## 6 Requirements For Providers Of High-Risk AI Systems

- **Risk management:** providers must implement a risk-management system covering the whole AI lifecycle.
- **Data and data governance:** training, validation, and test data must be properly governed and should be relevant, representative, free of errors, and complete.
- **Technical documentation:** documentation must be prepared before the system is placed on the market and kept up to date.
- **Record-keeping and logging:** the system must support traceability through logging.
- **Transparency and human oversight:** users must be able to interpret outputs, receive instructions for use, and the system must allow effective human oversight.
- **Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity:** the system must achieve an appropriate level of performance and be resilient against faults, errors, and attacks.

The slides also mention further provider duties such as conformity assessment, corrective action, a quality-management system, and registration in an EU database.

## 3 Requirements For Users Of High-Risk AI Systems

- **Use according to instructions:** users must operate the system in line with the instructions for use.
- **Relevant input data:** where users control the input data, they must ensure it is relevant for the intended purpose.
- **Monitor and report:** users must monitor the system, report risks or security incidents to the provider, and suspend use if necessary.

Overall, the AI Act combines innovation policy with rights protection: high-risk AI is not banned, but it is only allowed if strong governance, documentation, oversight, and safety requirements are met.
