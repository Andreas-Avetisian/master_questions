# Why does a "creative room" alone not trigger innovation? Please explain what it needs for innovation to happen

## Why a room alone is not enough

A creative room is just a visible artefact. The slides make the key point directly:

> People have lots of ideas, but you may need to create the **"space"** where innovation takes place. Just asking a question might be enough to get your people to come up with the answer.

"Space" here is not physical square-metres, it is cultural and behavioural space. Putting beanbags and a whiteboard in a room does nothing if people still follow the same mental path, are punished for failure, have no clear goal, or are not allowed to challenge the status quo.

Innovation is also difficult by nature:

- People like routine and follow the same mental path.
- Not every idea will lead to success (tension between **impact** and **feasibility**).
- Communication is hard: telling an idea and making others understand it the same way are two different things.

## What innovation actually needs

### 1. An innovation culture (Edgar Schein)

> "The only thing of real importance that leaders do is to create and manage culture. If you do not manage culture, it manages you."

Culture operates on three levels:

- **Artefacts** — visible structures and processes (the "creative room" lives here, and stops here if nothing else is built).
- **Espoused values** — stated strategies, goals, philosophies.
- **Basic assumptions** — the deeply held, taken-for-granted beliefs that actually drive behaviour.

### 2. Concrete conditions that create the culture

From the slide "How do we create Innovation Culture?":

- Clear goal setting.
- Assignments that match individual skills and provide positive challenge.
- Open communication systems.
- Frequent, constructive and supportive feedback.
- Equitable and generous rewards and recognition.
- Absence of unnecessary bureaucracy.
- Tolerance for failure, willingness to experiment.

### 3. Managing the paradox

An innovative culture is not just "freedom", it pairs opposites:

- Tolerance for failure **+** intolerance for incompetence.
- Willingness to experiment **+** high discipline.
- Collaboration **+** individual accountability.
- Freedom to speak up **+** brutal frankness.
- Flat structures **+** strong leadership.

### 4. The Innovator's DNA (individual-level skills)

Even the best culture needs people who actively drive innovation. Five skills:

1. **Questioning** — challenge the status quo.
2. **Observing** — notice what others miss.
3. **Experimenting** — try things out.
4. **Networking** — talk to people with opposite views / backgrounds.
5. **Association** — connect the unconnected; triggered by emotions, senses, prior inputs.

## Short summary

A creative room is only an **artefact**. Real innovation requires the layer below it: a managed **culture** (clear goals, open communication, tolerance for failure, low bureaucracy), leaders who hold the paradox (freedom + discipline), and individuals who practice the **Innovator's DNA** (questioning, observing, experimenting, networking, association). Without these, the room stays empty of ideas.
