# Describe the term "project specification" and its typical two major parts. What is a change request?

A **project specification** is a structured document that defines the project. It describes the objectives, scope, deliverables, requirements, and timeline, and acts as a common blueprint for everyone involved.

It is often divided into **two major parts**:

- **Requirements specification:** the **customer point of view**. It explains why the system is needed and what problem it is meant to solve.
- **Technical specification:** the **supplier point of view**. It describes the system from the supplier side and specifies what the system must do.

A **change request** is the formalization of an agreed change to the specification. It always refers to an existing specification and possibly previous change requests, so it requires numbering or versioning. If the change is large enough, it should lead to a new version of the specification.
