# What are the 6 criteria of Data Quality? Give an example for each one.

## The 6 criteria

**1. Completeness** : no required values are missing.

- *Example*: a customer table where every row has a non-empty email field; if 10 % of rows have a blank email, the data is incomplete.

**2. Accuracy** : the value correctly represents the real-world entity.

- *Example*: a customer's address in the CRM matches where they actually live; an address that points to an old, no-longer-valid flat is inaccurate, even though the field is filled.

**3. Consistency** : the same fact is stored the same way across systems and records.

- *Example*: the currency for an order is "EUR" in the billing DB and "EUR" in the analytics DB. If one system has "€" and another "EUR", the data is inconsistent.

**4. Timeliness** : data is up to date relative to the decision it supports.

- *Example*: a stock-level dashboard refreshed every 5 minutes for an online shop. A stock value from last week is not timely enough to prevent overselling.

**5. Validity** : values follow the defined format, type, and allowed range.

- *Example*: a date-of-birth field in `YYYY-MM-DD` with a year between 1900 and today; `1899-13-45` is invalid.

**6. Uniqueness** : each real-world entity appears exactly once, no duplicates.

- *Example*: one customer record per customer ID. If "Jane Doe" exists three times with slightly different spellings, uniqueness is violated.

## Short summary

The 6 classic data quality criteria are **Completeness, Accuracy, Consistency, Timeliness, Validity, Uniqueness**. Completeness and Validity answer *is the field filled and well-formed*, Accuracy answers *is it true*, Consistency and Uniqueness answer *is it aligned across the data set*, and Timeliness answers *is it still current*. A good data quality check ensures all six before the data is used for analysis or decision-making. (The AI course slide also adds Correctness, Relevance and Uniformity as further dimensions; the 6 above are the standard core set.)
