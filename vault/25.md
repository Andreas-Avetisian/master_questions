# Describe 3 different methods to collect insights during the "Empathy"-Stage in the Design Thinking Process. What are the strengths and weaknesses of each of these methods?

Three useful methods in the Empathy stage are `observation`, `individual depth interviews`, and `focus groups / customer communities`.

## 1. Observation
Example: `Fly on the Wall`, mystery shopping, eye-tracking.

Purpose:
Observe what people actually do in their real context without mainly relying on what they say.

Strengths:
- Captures real behavior instead of self-reported behavior.
- Useful when people cannot explain their actions well.
- Can reveal hidden routines, pain points, and context factors.
- Works even when language barriers exist.

Weaknesses:
- Shows `what` people do, but often not fully `why` they do it.
- Interpretation can be subjective.
- Hidden observation can raise ethical issues.
- Can be time-consuming to plan, document, and interpret.

## 2. Individual Depth Interview
Purpose:
Have a detailed one-on-one conversation to understand motives, language, pains, gains, and product usage patterns.

Strengths:
- Gives deep insight into motivations, attitudes, and needs.
- Good for understanding the user's own words and mental models.
- Flexible: the interviewer can ask follow-up questions.
- Especially useful for sensitive or complex topics.

Weaknesses:
- Answers depend on self-report, so people may forget, simplify, or present themselves positively.
- Interviewer bias can influence responses.
- Usually small samples, so results are not broadly generalizable.
- Preparation and analysis take effort.

## 3. Focus Group / Customer Community
Purpose:
Discuss experiences and opinions with several users together to explore shared themes and differences.

Strengths:
- Efficient way to collect many perspectives in one session.
- Group interaction can trigger ideas, memories, and reactions that would not appear in a solo interview.
- Helpful for identifying common pains, gains, and language.

Weaknesses:
- Dominant participants can influence the group.
- People may conform and hide unpopular opinions.
- Less depth per person than an individual interview.
- Sensitive topics may not be discussed openly.

## Short Comparison
- `Observation` is strongest for real behavior in context.
- `Depth interviews` are strongest for understanding motives and feelings.
- `Focus groups / customer communities` are strongest for collecting multiple viewpoints efficiently.

In an exam answer, a strong conclusion is: observation reveals behavior, interviews reveal motives, and focus groups reveal shared patterns and differences across users.
