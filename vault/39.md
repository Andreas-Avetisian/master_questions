# Critically examine the relationship between law and ethics. In your answer, clearly distinguish between the two concepts and explain their points of overlap and divergence.

Law and ethics are closely related, but they are not the same. Both are **normative disciplines** because they guide behaviour and deal with what ought to be done. However, law is a formal legal order created and enforced by the state, whereas ethics evaluates whether conduct is morally right or wrong.

**Distinction**
- **Law** consists of positive norms enacted by authorised state bodies in a formal procedure.
- It is backed by state coercion and sanctions such as fines or imprisonment.
- Following Max Weber, law is distinguished by a `Rechtsstab`, meaning an institutional apparatus that enforces compliance through coercion.
- **Ethics** is grounded in conscience, reason, and moral reflection rather than state enactment.
- Following Kant, ethics concerns a person's **inner attitude** and inner duty, while law mainly requires the **external conformity** of behaviour with legal rules.
- Ethical, religious, and social norms can also be sanctioned, but usually only informally through blame, repentance, or social disapproval.

**Overlap**
- Law and ethics overlap because legal systems often incorporate moral ideas.
- The slides explicitly connect law with values such as **justice, freedom, equality, and human dignity**.
- Ethics therefore influences both the content of legal norms and their interpretation, especially in constitutional and human-rights law.

**Divergence**
- What is lawful is not always ethical, and what is ethical is not always lawful.
- The clearest example in the slides is **apartheid**: it was legally permitted, but morally wrong.
- This shows that formal legal validity does not automatically guarantee moral legitimacy.
- Radbruch's formula sharpens this point: law that is **intolerably unjust** and denies the basic equality of all persons may count as **legal injustice**.

**Critical Assessment**
- The relationship is best understood as one of **partial overlap**, not identity.
- A separation between law and ethics is important because law needs legal certainty, formal validity, and enforceability.
- But a complete separation is dangerous, because it can make deeply unjust rules appear acceptable merely because they were validly enacted.
- Ethics therefore acts as a critical standard for judging law, while law gives some ethical principles institutional and enforceable form.

**Exam Formula**
Law and ethics are both normative disciplines, but law is state-made and coercively enforced, while ethics is grounded in conscience and moral reasoning; they overlap in values such as justice and human dignity, yet diverge because legality and morality do not always coincide.
