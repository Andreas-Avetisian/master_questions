# What is the difference between cognitive and statistical bias? Name examples for both.

## The two definitions, straight from the slide "What is Bias?"

The deck says bias is "difficult to define strictly" and splits it along disciplinary lines:

- **Cognitive bias (social sciences)**: *"subjective or otherwise unjustified judgement which makes a decision or assessment unfair or problematic in some way (cf. the Cognitive Bias Codex)."* It sits in the head of the person judging: a systematic deviation of human reasoning from rationality.
- **Statistical bias (statistics / data science)**: *"unrepresentative data, distortion in the data, data base is not a good representation of the facts that they (are intended to) describe or reflect."* It sits in the data: the sample or measurement is skewed relative to the reality it is supposed to represent.

So the key difference is the **locus**: cognitive bias is a property of *human judgement*, statistical bias is a property of *data / sampling / measurement*. Both can feed into algorithmic bias, which is why the lecture introduces them together before discussing fairness and discrimination.

## Examples for cognitive bias (slide "Examples for Cognitive Bias")

- **Anchoring effect**: information in the vicinity, even if irrelevant, influences decisions (salary negotiations, price valuations).
- **IKEA effect**: self-designed or self-built objects are estimated to have higher subjective value than they objectively warrant.
- **Mere exposure effect**: repeated perception of a person, object or phenomenon leads to more positive opinions about it.

## Examples for statistical bias (slide "Examples for Statistical Bias")

- **Social desirability**: in surveys, people give answers they expect to be socially acceptable, so the data no longer reflects real attitudes.
- **Base rate fallacy**: in rare phenomena even a low false-positive rate produces more false than true positives (rare-disease diagnosis, anti-terrorism measures).
- **Lead time bias**: earlier detection of a disease makes survival time look longer without actually extending life.
- **Survivorship bias**: only cases that passed some filter are considered (classic WW2 bomber example, successful business case studies, academic careers).

## Short summary

**Cognitive bias** is a *human reasoning* bias: a systematic, often unconscious distortion of judgement (anchoring effect, IKEA effect, mere exposure effect). **Statistical bias** is a *data* bias: the sample or measurement is unrepresentative of reality (social desirability, base rate fallacy, lead time bias, survivorship bias). Cognitive bias lives in heads, statistical bias lives in datasets, and algorithmic systems can inherit both.
