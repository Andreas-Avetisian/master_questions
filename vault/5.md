# What's the difference between non-random sampling methods and random sampling methods? Explain one non-random sampling method in more detail.

**Difference**

- `Random sampling`: selection is based on chance. In a true random sample, each person in the population has the same chance of being selected.
- `Non-random sampling`: people enter the sample because the researcher makes a systematic decision about who is included.
- Random sampling usually has higher representativeness and a lower risk of bias.
- Non-random sampling is often easier and faster, but the risk of bias is higher and the results may represent the population less well.

**Quota Sampling**

- `Quota sampling` is a non-random method.
- The idea is to make the sample reflect important structural characteristics of the population.
- Typical quota criteria are `age`, `gender`, `residence`, `education`, or `knowledge about a topic`.

1. Determine the relevant characteristics.
2. Find their proportions in the population.
3. Convert these proportions into target numbers for the sample.
4. Recruit participants until each quota is filled.

- Example: if the population is `50.84%` women and `49.16%` men and the sample size is `100`, the quota would be `51` women and `49` men.
- Quotas can also combine multiple criteria, for example `gender + age`.
- Advantage: faster and more practical than true random sampling.
- Problem: even if the quota structure matches the population, the actual people within each quota are still not chosen randomly, so bias can remain.

**Exam Ready**

- `Random sampling = chance-based selection with equal selection probability.`
- `Non-random sampling = researcher-controlled selection based on a systematic decision.`
- `Quota sampling` is a good example of non-random sampling: the sample is built to match population proportions, but the individuals are not selected by true random choice.
