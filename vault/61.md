# Describe the related work section in a funding proposal. What are the differences to an academic literature review?

In a funding proposal, the related work or **state-of-the-art** section is short, selective, and strongly purpose-driven. Its job is not completeness, but to show that the proposed project is **new, needed, and well positioned**.

## In A Funding Proposal

- usually only **2-3 pages**
- short and precise
- reduced to the actually important parts
- structured along the proposal itself, for example objectives, deliverables, or sub-targets
- meant to show the **gap** the proposal will solve
- often focused mainly on academic work, although industrial or NGO work can also matter

## Difference To An Academic Literature Review

- A proposal SotA is **selective**; an academic literature review aims to be **comprehensive**.
- A proposal SotA argues **why the approach is new and needed**; a literature review maps and compares the field more broadly.
- A proposal SotA follows the logic of the proposal; a literature review is structured around systematic comparison of approaches.
- A proposal SotA is short; a literature review can be extensive.
- A proposal SotA often spans several related fields if needed for the proposal; a literature review usually focuses more clearly on one field.

## Exam Formula

The related work section in a funding proposal is a short, targeted state-of-the-art overview that shows why the project is novel and necessary. An academic literature review is much more comprehensive and mainly aims to compare and organise the state of research in a field.
