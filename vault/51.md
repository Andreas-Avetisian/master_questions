# Explain the steps in the typical review process. What type of review strategies do you know?

A typical review process starts before the paper is actually read in detail. First, the chairs define topics in the system, reviewers select topics, and conflicts of interest are considered. Then papers are submitted, reviewers may bid on papers, and the papers are assigned, usually to at least three reviewers.

## Typical Review Process

1. **Topic setup and reviewer matching:** PC chairs enter topics, reviewers select topics, and conflicts of interest are declared.
2. **Submission and bidding:** papers are submitted and reviewers can often bid on papers they want, cannot, or must not review.
3. **Paper assignment and deadlines:** the organisers assign papers and set deadlines.
4. **Reviewing:** reviewers assess contribution, correctness, evaluation, presentation quality, and possible misconduct such as plagiarism.
5. **Comments and discussion:** reviews are submitted, comments may be opened, and reviewers discuss shortcomings, often with a lead reviewer.
6. **Decision:** the venue decides on rejection, acceptance, or sometimes revision; some conferences use **shepherding** for conditionally accepted papers.
7. **Camera-ready:** accepted papers are revised into the final version.

The PM1 slides also stress a practical review method: read abstract and introduction first, identify the claimed contribution, compare it to the conclusion and the state of the art, then check claim by claim whether the paper actually delivers.

## Review Strategies / Variants

- **Single-blind review:** the reviewer knows the authors, but the authors do not know the reviewer.
- **Double-blind review:** both sides are anonymized.
- **Visible vs. restricted reviews:** reviews may be visible to all reviewers or only to those reviewing the same paper.
- **Reviewer anonymity vs. known-to-reviewers:** reviewers may stay anonymous or be visible to the other reviewers.
- **Commenting and discussion-based review:** reviewers can comment on each other's reviews and discuss weaknesses before the final decision.
- **Shepherded acceptance:** a paper is accepted conditionally and improved under guidance before the camera-ready version.

## Short Conclusion

The core pattern is assignment, review, discussion, and decision. The main strategy distinction in the cited slides is **single-blind vs. double-blind**, but the process can also vary through review visibility, reviewer discussion, and shepherding.
